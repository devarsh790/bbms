Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about blood donation

Eligibility

Who can donate blood?

Anyone who is fit and healthy, weighs at least 50kg (110 lbs), and is between 17-66 years old can donate blood. If you're over 70, you can donate if you've given blood in the last 2 years.

How often can I donate?

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). For platelet donations, you can donate every 7 days, up to 24 times per year.

Can I donate if I have a tattoo?

Yes, but you must wait 3 months after getting a tattoo before donating blood to ensure there's no risk of infection.

Donation Process

How long does it take to donate blood?

The entire process takes about 45-60 minutes, including registration, health screening, and the actual donation. The blood collection itself only takes 8-10 minutes.

Does it hurt?

You may feel a slight pinch when the needle is inserted, but most donors report minimal discomfort. The process is generally painless.

What should I do before donating?

Eat a healthy meal, drink plenty of water, get a good night's sleep, and bring a valid ID. Avoid fatty foods before donation.

Safety & Health

Is donating blood safe?

Yes, absolutely! We use sterile, single-use needles and equipment. There is no risk of contracting any disease from donating blood.

Will I feel weak after donating?

Most people feel fine after donating. We recommend resting for 10-15 minutes and having refreshments. Your body replaces the donated blood volume within 24-48 hours.

What happens to my blood after donation?

Your blood is tested for blood type and infectious diseases, then separated into components (red cells, plasma, platelets) and stored until needed by patients.

Other Questions

Can I donate if I'm taking medication?

It depends on the medication. Most common medications don't prevent donation, but some do. Please inform our staff about any medications you're taking.

What if I don't know my blood type?

That's perfectly fine! We'll test your blood type when you donate, and we'll let you know what it is.

Can I donate if I've traveled recently?

Travel to certain countries may require a waiting period before you can donate. Please check with our staff about specific travel restrictions.
