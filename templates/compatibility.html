Key Facts

Universal Donor

O- blood type can be given to anyone, making it extremely valuable in emergencies when there's no time to determine the patient's blood type.

Universal Recipient

AB+ blood type can receive from any blood type, but can only donate to other AB+ individuals.

Rh Factor

The + or - indicates the Rh factor. Rh- blood can be given to both Rh+ and Rh- patients, but Rh+ can only be given to Rh+ patients.

Inheritance

Blood type is inherited from parents. Each parent contributes one of two ABO genes to their child.

Rare Blood Types

AB- is the rarest blood type, found in only 1% of the population. Donors with rare types are especially valuable!

High Demand

O- blood is always in high demand because it can be used for any patient in emergency situations.

Most Common

O+ is the most common blood type, but high demand means we still need regular donations!

Understanding Blood Components

Red Blood Cells

Carry oxygen throughout the body. Used for trauma patients, surgery, and anemia treatment.

Plasma

The liquid portion of blood. Used for burn victims, trauma patients, and clotting disorders.

Platelets

Help blood clot. Critical for cancer patients, transplant recipients, and trauma victims.
